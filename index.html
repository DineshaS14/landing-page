Projects

FoodTrackrApp

Kotlin | RESTful-API | Android Studio | Gradle
Developed an app with 3 other members that allows users to log their calorie intake, track their progress, and receive notifications about their goals. As project manager, I led a team of four from wire-framing through to app development. The app features RecyclerView, ConstraintLayout, and multi-screen data transfer to enhance user experience and functionality.

  • Category: Health and Fitness
  • Mobile: Indispensable for tracking fitness goals on the go. Users can log food and exercise immediately, ensuring accuracy and convenience.
  • Story: LifeGoalTracker empowers users to set and achieve fitness goals through meticulous tracking and calculations of caloric intake and expenditure.
  • Market: Designed for fitness enthusiasts, individuals aiming for weight management, and anyone seeking a structured and quantifiable approach to their health goals.
  • Habit: Intended for daily use, with users updating their food intake and exercise output regularly for real-time insights.
  • Scope: V1 offers basic functionality to set goals, log food and exercise, and track remaining calories. Future versions could introduce a database of foods and exercises, integration with wearable devices, and an AI-based suggestion system for meal and workout plans.

TokyoOlympicPrediction

Python | Kaggle-dataset | Google Colab | Numpy | plotly | Seaborn
This project demonstrates the application of data analytics to understand and predict Olympic performance. By using historical data and advanced modeling techniques, the analysis provides insights into the factors influencing medal outcomes and offers predictions for future Olympic events. Future enhancements could include adding more variables or refining models for better prediction accuracy.

Project Details:

    Data Preparation and Cleaning: Load data from Excel files, convert to CSV, and clean it to remove duplicates and handle missing values.
    Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA): Use visualizations and statistical analyses to uncover patterns, such as total medal distributions and correlations between variables.
    Predictive Modeling: Build regression models using aggregated data to predict medal counts in upcoming Olympics.
    Conclusion: Summarize insights and predictions, highlighting the project's findings and implications for understanding Olympic performance.
